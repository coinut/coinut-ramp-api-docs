A deposit address is a Coinut-generated receiving address — where a customer sends crypto to trigger an offramp. It's created automatically when you call [`POST /autoramp-flow/offramp`](/api-reference/autoramp-flow/create-an-offramp-flow-with-deposit-address) <Icon icon="link" size={14} /> (see [Autoramp Flows](/transactions/autoramp-flows)), or directly via [`POST /deposit-address/create`](/api-reference/deposit-address/create-a-deposit-address) <Icon icon="link" size={14} />.

## Status

Unlike crypto addresses (which are customer-owned and usable immediately), deposit addresses go through an **approval step** and carry a `status`:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `PENDING` | Just created — `address` is **not yet populated** |
| `VERIFIED` | Approved — `address` is populated and safe to display |
| `REJECTED` | Rejected — do not display, contact support |
| `DELETED` | Removed |

<Warning>
  A newly created deposit address starts `PENDING` with `address: null`. It is **not** available synchronously — poll [`GET /deposit-address/detail`](/api-reference/deposit-address/get-deposit-address-detail) <Icon icon="link" size={14} /> or listen for the `DEPOSIT_ADDRESS_APPROVED` webhook, and only show the address to the customer once `status` leaves `PENDING` and `address` is populated.
</Warning>

Supported networks: `ETH` and `TRX` today. `MATIC` is accepted by the schema but not yet supported end-to-end — don't offer it to customers.
