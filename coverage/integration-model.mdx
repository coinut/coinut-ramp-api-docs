title: "Integration Models"
description: "The three ways to integrate Coinut Partner Ramp, based on who ultimately transacts: your own company, your business clients, or your individual users."
---

Coinut Partner Ramp fits three patterns, distinguished by **who ultimately moves money** through the rails: your own company, the businesses you serve, or the individual users of your product. All three run on the same signed HTTPS API — what changes is who the end customer is and how they are onboarded.

## At a Glance

| Model | Who transacts on Coinut | Example | Integration |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Direct (B2B)** | Your own company | A fintech settling treasury between fiat and stablecoins | API |
| **Embedded — Business (B2B2B)** | Your business clients | A payments platform routing stablecoin payouts to its merchants | API |
| **Embedded — Consumer (B2B2C)** | Your individual users | A wallet app letting users onramp fiat into crypto | API |

<Note>
  Coinut Partner Ramp is API-first. You keep full ownership of the customer-facing experience and drive everything through the endpoints described across this documentation — there is no Coinut-hosted white-label frontend to embed. A partner dashboard is on the roadmap but not yet part of the integration surface.
</Note>

## Direct (B2B): Using the Rails Yourself

Run Coinut against your own operations — treasury moves, settlement, and payouts — without onboarding any downstream customers.

**Where this fits:**

- Converting a stablecoin balance into fiat and paying it out to your own bank accounts
- Settling exchange or platform balances between crypto and fiat via [Offramp](/guides/offramp-integration)
- Holding and moving value across fiat and stablecoins through [Virtual Accounts](/virtual-accounts/virtual-accounts-guide)

<Note>
  These models are not mutually exclusive. The same company can transact for itself under Direct and, at the same time, embed Coinut to serve its own clients under one of the models below.
</Note>

## Embedded — Business (B2B2B): Serving Business Clients

Build Coinut into your product so that **your business clients** transact. You act as the partner; each client you onboard becomes a Coinut customer in its own right.

**Where this fits:**

- A payment provider offering stablecoin transfers to merchant clients
- A banking-style platform issuing fiat receiving accounts to downstream businesses
- A payroll product paying contractors on behalf of company clients

Each business client is created with `isIndividual: false`, which routes onboarding through corporate (KYB) verification. Once confirmed, the client can use the same onramp, offramp, virtual account, and payout capabilities available on the API.

## Embedded — Consumer (B2B2C): Serving Individual Users

Build Coinut into a consumer product so that **your individual users** transact. Again you are the partner, and each user becomes a Coinut customer.

**Where this fits:**

- A neobank issuing named fiat receiving accounts to retail users
- A wallet letting users onramp fiat into stablecoins
- A remittance app where senders move stablecoins and recipients cash out to a bank account

Individual users are created with `isIndividual: true` and onboarded through Sumsub. Coinut supports two ways to complete that verification:

| Approach | How it works |
| --- | --- |
| Hosted verification | Omit `sumsubShareToken` on `POST /customer/create` and Coinut returns a `sumsubLink` your user completes directly. |
| Shared KYC token | Pass an existing `sumsubShareToken` and Coinut pulls the verification data through the Sumsub API instead of hosting a new flow. |

See [Customer Lifecycle](/customers/customer-lifecycle) for the full onboarding and status model.

## Customer Classification

Whichever model you run, every customer created via `POST /customer/create` carries two independent fields that determine onboarding and behavior:

| Field | Values | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `type` | `DEPOSITOR`, `PAYEE` | Enum on `CustomerTypeEnum`, defaults to `DEPOSITOR`. Every documented flow (onramp, offramp) uses `DEPOSITOR`; `PAYEE` exists in the schema but has no distinct documented behavior yet — confirm its intended use with Coinut before relying on it. |
| `isIndividual` | `true`, `false` | Individual (KYC) vs corporate (KYB) customer, defaults to `true`. It sets the verification depth on the Sumsub side, not which APIs are available. |

```json
{
  "name": "John Doe",
  "email": "john@example.com",
  "type": "DEPOSITOR",
  "isIndividual": true,
  "externalId": "user_12345"
}
```

## How You Drive the Flow

Independent of which model you run, you choose how much of the sequencing Coinut handles:

- **Autoramp** — `POST /autoramp-flow/onramp` and `POST /autoramp-flow/offramp` bundle the fiat/crypto entry point, KYC gating, and settlement leg into a single flow. Fastest path to live, best suited to individual users.
- **Direct API orchestration** — call the underlying resource endpoints yourself (`/virtual-account/create`, `/bank-account/add`, `/crypto-address/add`, `/deposit-address/create`, `/payment/create`) and manage the sequencing, retries, and customer aggregation. Suited to platforms with pooled liquidity or bespoke payout logic.

<Note>
  "Autoramp" maps directly to the `/autoramp-flow/*` endpoints in the OpenAPI contract. "Direct API orchestration" is a narrative label for calling the underlying resource APIs one by one — it is not a literal enum value in the schema. Confirm the specifics of any pooled or enterprise setup with Coinut before committing to an architecture.
</Note>

## Choosing a Model

- **Consumer app, individual users** → Embedded (B2B2C), `isIndividual: true`, usually via Autoramp
- **Serving business clients** → Embedded (B2B2B), `isIndividual: false`, often Direct API orchestration for bespoke sequencing
- **Your own treasury or settlement** → Direct (B2B), typically Direct API orchestration with `isIndividual: false`
