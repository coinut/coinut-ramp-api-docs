A bank account is the fiat-side destination for an offramp payout — where converted crypto is deposited as fiat.

## Adding a Bank Account

Call [`POST /bank-account/add`](/api-reference/bank-account/add-customers-bank-account) <Icon icon="link" size={14} />:

```json
{
  --form customerId=6ac34182-aa2e-4290-ab1b-302a09f451d1 \
  --form currency=USD \
  --form 'bankName=Chase Bank' \
  --form accountNo=1234567890 \
  --form swift=CHASUS33 \
  --form bankCountry=USA \
  --form 'bankAddress=270 Park Ave, New York, NY 10017' \
  --form aba=021000021 \
  --form iban=GB33BUKB20201555555555 \
  --form 'beneficiaryName=John Doe' \
  --form 'beneficiaryAddress=1 Infinite Loop, Cupertino, CA 95014' \
  --form 'interBankName=Citibank N.A.' \
  --form interBankBic=CITIUS33 \
  --form interBankAccountNo=9876543210 \
  --form institutionNumber=001 \
  --form transit=00011 \
  --form 'label=Main USD settlement account' \
  --form statementFile='@example-file'
}
```

The API only strictly requires `customerId`, `currency`, and `bankName` — it does not enforce currency-specific combinations of the remaining fields at the schema level. Still, supply the fields your customer's currency needs below; payouts can be delayed or fail at the banking layer if the receiving bank needs a field the schema didn't require you to send.

`currency` accepts only `GBP`, `EUR`, `AUD`, `SGD`, or `USD` — any other value is rejected.

| Currency | Recommended Fields | Optional Fields | Typical Settlement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| USD | `bankName`, `accountNo`, `aba` and/or `swift` | `beneficiaryName`, `bankAddress` | 1-2 business days |
| EUR | `bankName`, `iban`, `swift` | `beneficiaryName`, `bankAddress`, `accountNo` | 1 business day |
| GBP | `bankName`, `iban`, `swift` | `beneficiaryName`, `bankAddress`, `accountNo` | 1 business day |
| AUD | `bankName`, `accountNo` | `beneficiaryName` | 1 business day |
| SGD | `bankName`, `accountNo`, `swift` | `beneficiaryName`, `bankAddress` | 1 business day |

{/*
<Note>
  There is no `sortCode`, `bsb`, or `accountName` field on this endpoint — the field for the account holder's name is `beneficiaryName`. If your AUD payout needs a BSB captured explicitly, check with Coinut support on current formatting expectations before going live.
</Note>
*/}


### Additional fields

For intermediary-bank routing or region-specific rails, the endpoint also accepts: `bankCountry`, `beneficiaryAddress`, `interBankName`, `interBankBic`, `interBankAccountNo`, `institutionNumber`, `transit`, `label`, and an optional `statementFile` upload (multipart).

Store the returned `id` — it's the value you pass as `to` when creating an offramp flow (see [Autoramp Flows](/transactions/autoramp-flows)).

{/*
<Note>
  Banking cutoff times vary by currency and institution. EUR SEPA transfers typically process until 3:00 PM CET; USD ACH transfers have a 5:00 PM ET cutoff. Communicate these timelines to your customers.
</Note>
*/}
